Tell us About You

Tell us about a time when you started something from scratch (a business, a project, a program, etc.) and one impressive achievement you are proud of from this experience.

Past Experience

My brother-in-law and I once started a subscription based food delivery company that served students at York University. We started with $30 in capital and by the end of 4 months we had earned over $4000. Although we earned money, it was more of a learning experience than something to brag about.

Our business started by first advertising with flyers that we designed ourselves, and Kijiji advertisements. From this I learned what kinds of things attract people, and a little bit of SEO where learning to use good keywords attracted significantly more customers.

Then once we moved onto managing orders we began to learn more about modularization and scalability. Our first mistakes with large orders taught us that dividing individual tasks to separate people is essential to increasing efficiency, and reducing time wastage. We also learned about designing for scalability, because we were using text messages to keep track of and manage orders, mistakes were possible, and very easy to make.

In the end we learned from our mistakes, and became smarter than we were before our venture, and wee gained many insights into how best to move forward if we ever tried something like that again.

Briefly tell us about one transformative past failure.

Failure

One of the biggest transformative failures I've had was all due to my inattention, underpreparedness and egocentricity. I had applied for a paid hospital internship at the LTRI of the University of Toronto, this was something I was only moderately interested in, and even that only for the money. Other students interested in health sciences from my school had also applied however they never heard back. I became one of 15 people selected for ten positions, at this I began to feed my ego, and disregarded preparation for the interview, due to this I was rejected.

This rejection opened my eyes, and I began to search elsewhere for an internship, unpaid of course, I had lost the opportunity. The search consumed me, and I began speaking to professors at York University for an internship, and I found one with Dr.Elder.
